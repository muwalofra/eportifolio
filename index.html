Information Security Management

In this module we looked at the various areas where security and risk overlap and interact with each other. We reviewed the various methods of risk assessment â€“ looking at both qualitative and quantitative methods. We discussed a number of more traditional risk models (such as STRIDE, DREAD and Attack Trees) as well as reviewing the increasing trend towards hybrid models. We also reviewed common security standards such as PCI-DSS and look at business continuity and disaster recovery solutions. We also discussed some of the key research topics and advances driving the fields of security and risk management. The main topics in this module include;

1. The difference between qualitative and quantitative risk assessments.

2. Describing how to carry out qualitative assessments.

3. Explaining what is meant by and how to create threat models.

The module outcomes of include;

1. Identify and analyse security risks, threats and vulnerabilities in information systems and determine appropriate methodologies, tools, and techniques to manage and/or solve them.

2. Gather and synthesise information from multiple sources (including internet security alerts & warning sites) to aid in the systematic analysis of security breaches & issues.

3. Critically appraise and utilise methodologies, tools and techniques that help manage and audit security issues.

4. Articulate the legal, social, ethical, and professional issues faced by information security professionals.
